About

I am a PhD student at National Taiwan University, working at the NTU Robot Learning Lab under the supervision of Prof. Shao-Hua Sun. I also completed my master's and bachelor's degrees at NTU.

My research centers on enabling robots to learn complex behaviors through interaction with the physical world. I believe that robots need to actively interact with their environment in order to collect the right information for learning effective and generalizable skills.

Research Interests

🤖

Robot Learning

Developing methods that enable robots to acquire manipulation and locomotion skills from experience, bridging the gap between simulation and the real world.

🧩

Skill-Based Reinforcement Learning

Designing hierarchical frameworks that decompose complex tasks into reusable skill primitives, enabling efficient learning and compositional generalization.
